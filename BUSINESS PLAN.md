## 1. Executive Summary

3D Print You turns a customer's photo into a personalized, collectible 3D-printed figurine through a done-for-you workflow: pick a limited style, upload a photo, approve an AI-generated 2D concept, add a name for the base, and pay. The customer never touches a printer, slicer, or 3D file.

The market timing is strong. The global figurines market is ~**$10.2B in 2026, growing to ~$24.1B by 2036 (8.9% CAGR)**, and the broader personalized-gifts market is ~**$31.4B in 2025 → ~$57.6B by 2034 (7% CAGR)**. Personalized figurines show **~35% higher repeat-purchase rates** than generic products, and the "AI action figure" trend has already generated **890,000+ TikTok videos** — proof of massive top-of-funnel demand that is currently being captured mostly by *free digital* tools, not physical products. That gap — huge desire, almost no one delivering a quality physical object done-for-you — is the opening.

The core strategic recommendation of this plan:

- **Sell two customer-facing tiers only: Unpainted and Painted.** Keep the storefront simple at launch, exactly as you want.
- **Position as a premium collectible, not a commodity.** Recommended launch prices: **Unpainted $59**, **Painted $129** — a 2.2× ratio that mirrors Hero Forge's own painted-vs-uncolored premium (2.25×) and sits *below* mini-me statue competitors ($130–$690), so you look like a smart-value premium buy.
- **Lead paid acquisition with the Painted hero product, not the cheap tier.** At industry median ad costs, a $59 product cannot profitably absorb customer-acquisition cost on the first order; the $129 product can. This is the single most important unit-economics decision in the plan.
- **Use the Unpainted tier as a low-friction entry + affiliate engine.** Unpainted buyers land on a thank-you page with Amazon affiliate links to the exact paints and brushes they need — extra margin at zero inventory cost, and it turns a budget buyer into an engaged DIY hobbyist likely to reorder.
- **Optimize the Painted cost on the back end, invisibly to the customer.** Negotiate PolyX3D toward a *partial-color print + artist detailing* method (machine lays base colors, artist finishes face/eyes/highlights) instead of full hand-painting from raw plastic. This cuts skilled paint hours and raises throughput without changing what the customer sees or pays.

## 2. Ideal Customer Profiles (ICP)

Personalized figurines are almost always bought as **gifts or identity/keepsake pieces**, and the emotional trigger — not the object — is what converts. The four profiles below are ranked by expected launch value.

| # | Profile | Who they are | Trigger / occasion | Tier they buy | Why they convert |
|---|---------|--------------|--------------------|---------------|------------------|
| 1 | **The Sentimental Gifter** | 28–55, mostly women, buying for a partner, parent, or child | Birthday, anniversary, Father's/Mother's Day, graduation, memorial | **Painted** ($129) | Wants a "wow," keepsake-quality gift with a face they recognize; price-tolerant when it's emotional |
| 2 | **The Fandom / Gamer Self-Styler** | 18–35, gamers, cosplayers, D&D and anime fans | "Turn myself into a hero," collectible drops, streamer merch | Painted or Unpainted | Loves the limited-style-drop mechanic; identity expression; follows the AI-action-figure trend |
| 3 | **The Hobbyist / DIY Painter** | 20–45, tabletop & mini-painting hobbyists | Wants a custom base mini to paint themselves | **Unpainted** ($59) + affiliate | Buys the raw print cheaply, then buys paints/brushes via your affiliate links; high reorder potential |
| 4 | **The Occasion / Team Buyer** | 30–55, HR, small-business owners, coaches, event planners | Retirement, employee recognition, sports team, wedding party | Painted, multi-unit | Higher AOV via multi-figure orders; predictable seasonal demand |

**Positioning implication:** ICP #1 funds the business (highest margin, price-tolerant, ad-responsive). ICP #3 is the affiliate/repeat engine and a cheap way to build community and user-generated content. Early ad creative should speak to ICP #1's emotional gifting moment while the limited-drop mechanic pulls in ICP #2.

## 4. Pricing Strategy

### 4.1 The two-tier retail model (what the customer sees)

Keep it simple. Two tiers, one clear upgrade decision.

| Tier | Name idea | Launch price | Test band | What it is |
|------|-----------|-------------:|-----------|------------|
| **Entry** | "Raw" / DIY-ready | **$59** | $49–$69 | Single-color print + named base. Clean, paintable, collectible as-is. |
| **Hero** | "Signature" / hand-finished | **$129** | $109–$149 | Full-color, hand-finished figurine. The gift-quality product. |

The **$129 : $59 = 2.19× ratio** is deliberate. It closely matches Hero Forge's own 2.25× premium for adding color, so the painted upcharge is anchored in what the market has already trained buyers to accept. Show both side by side; the Painted tier should always be visually presented as the "most popular / best value" default.

### 4.2 Evidence for the painted-vs-unpainted price gap

You asked specifically what evidence supports a price range between painted and unpainted. The research is consistent:

- **Hero Forge** charges **$44.99 painted-color vs $19.99 uncolored** — a **+$25 / +125% premium (2.25×)** on the identical model. This is the cleanest apples-to-apples benchmark available.
- **Miniature painting commissions** price the *labor alone* at **$4–6.50 per figure (budget)** up to **$30–80+ (display/premium)**. Painting is a genuine, market-priced skilled service — customers already understand they pay more for it.
- **Bobblehead services** (fully painted, hand-finished) sit at **$50–148**, validating that a hand-finished personalized figure in the ~$100–130 range is normal, not aggressive.
- **Full statue-from-photo services** start at **$130–170+**, so a $129 painted piece is *under market* for done-for-you photo figurines.

**Conclusion:** a painted premium of roughly **+100% to +150% over the unpainted price is well-supported by the market.** Your $59 → $129 spread lands squarely inside that evidence-based band.

### 4.3 Unit economics (per order, before ad cost)

Based on PolyX3D quotes plus estimated landed costs (US shipping ~$8–9, provider/API ~$3.50, packaging ~$3–4, payment fees 2.9% + $0.30).

| Tier | Production cost | Shipping | API | Packaging | Payment fee | **Landed COGS** | Price | **Contribution** | Margin |
|------|---------------:|--------:|----:|----------:|-----------:|----------------:|------:|-----------------:|-------:|
| Unpainted $59 | $15.50 | $8.00 | $3.50 | $3.00 | $2.01 | **$32.01** | $59 | **$26.99** | 46% |
| Unpainted $69 | $15.50 | $8.00 | $3.50 | $3.00 | $2.30 | $32.30 | $69 | $36.70 | 53% |
| Painted $129 | $55.00* | $9.00 | $3.50 | $4.00 | $4.04 | **$75.54** | $129 | **$53.46** | 41% |
| Painted $149 | $55.00* | $9.00 | $3.50 | $4.00 | $4.62 | $76.12 | $149 | $72.88 | 49% |

*Painted production assumes a **negotiated $55 target** via the partial-color-plus-detailing method (see 4.5). At full hand-paint (~$60.50) contribution drops ~$5.50; still positive at $129.

### 4.4 The affiliate DIY package (Unpainted upsell)

Every Unpainted buyer hits a thank-you page with Amazon affiliate links to a curated "finish-your-figurine" kit: a recommended acrylic paint set, 2–3 brush sizes, primer, and a matte sealer.

- Amazon Associates pays roughly **3% on tools/home-improvement and up to 5% on handmade/crafts** categories. A typical paint-plus-brush basket of **$30–50** yields **~$1–2.50 per converting buyer**.
- This is modest per order but **pure margin at zero cost or inventory**, and strategically it does more than earn commission: it converts a budget buyer into an *engaged hobbyist* who shares results (free UGC) and is far more likely to reorder. Track it, but don't build the model's survival on it.
- **Recommendation:** launch this as a simple curated link block. Only graduate to a branded paint kit (your own SKU) once volume justifies holding inventory.

### 4.5 Back-end cost optimization (invisible to the customer)

This is your margin lever, negotiated with PolyX3D — the customer never sees it.

| Method | Print cost | Paint labor | Materials | **Total** | Throughput |
|--------|----------:|------------:|----------:|----------:|-----------|
| A — Single-color + full hand-paint (2 hr) | $15.50 | $40.00 | $5.00 | **$60.50** | Slow (skilled hours) |
| B — Multicolor base + 1 hr detailing | $37.50 | $20.00 | $5.00 | **$62.50** | Faster |
| C — **Negotiated partial-color + light detailing (~0.75 hr)** | $37.50 | $15.00 | $5.00 | **$57.50** | Fastest, most scalable |

Your instinct is correct: **paying more for a multicolor print that already lays down the base colors, then having the artist finish only the details, trades a small print-cost increase for a large reduction in skilled labor hours.** The per-unit cost is similar to full hand-painting, but the *throughput* win is decisive — an artist finishing details in 45 minutes instead of painting from scratch in 2 hours can process 2.5× more orders per day. That is how you scale the Painted tier without a hiring bottleneck. **Action: negotiate Method C pricing with PolyX3D and confirm on samples before committing.**

### 4.6 Average-order-value (AOV) lifters

Two tiers keep the store simple, but offer optional add-ons at checkout to lift AOV without adding storefront complexity:

| Add-on | Suggested price | Est. cost | Notes |
|--------|---------------:|----------:|-------|
| Larger size (~200mm) | +$40 | +$12–18 | Strong margin; premium buyers self-select |
| Rush production (5-day) | +$25 | ~$0 | Near-pure margin; capacity permitting |
| Premium gift box + card | +$12 | +$4 | High perceived value for gifters (ICP #1) |
| Additional figure (couple / pet / family) | +$99 painted / +$45 unpainted | as per tier | Biggest AOV lever; couples & families are top gift use cases |
| Digital keepsake (concept art file / AR) | +$9 | ~$0 | Pure-margin digital add-on |

Target: ~15–20% add-on attach lifts blended AOV from ~$97 to ~$110+, which materially improves the ad-payback math below.

## 5. Three-Year Projections — Financials + Funnel (the "EDIAP" ask)

- Mix shifts from 45% Painted / 55% Unpainted at launch toward Painted over time; blended AOV rises $100 → $120 as add-ons and mix improve.
- Blended landed COGS ≈ 54–56% of AOV.
- Meta/Facebook is the primary paid channel. Industry medians used: **CPM ~$13, CPC ~$1.11, ecommerce conversion ~1.57%, median cost-per-purchase ~$38** (improving as creative/audience mature). Organic/referral share of orders grows over time, lowering blended CAC.

## 6. SWOT Analysis

| **Strengths** | **Weaknesses** |
|---------------|----------------|
| Done-for-you workflow — no printer/skill needed | Thin first-order margin on the Unpainted tier vs. ad cost |
| Approve-before-pay 2D proof de-risks purchase & saves provider cost | Painting is a labor/throughput bottleneck until Method C is proven |
| Local partner (PolyX3D) → speed, quality control, trust | Single-partner dependency (capacity, pricing, quality risk) |
| Limited-drop scarcity drives engagement & collectibility | New brand, no reviews or social proof yet |
| Premium-value price position under statue competitors | Founder-run; limited bandwidth for ops + marketing + fulfillment |

| **Opportunities** | **Threats** |
|-------------------|-------------|
| Massive AI-action-figure demand with almost no quality *physical* fulfillment | Rising Meta ad costs (CPM +20% in 2025) squeeze CAC |
| Growing personalized-gifts & figurine markets (7–8.9% CAGR) | Low-cost overseas & Etsy sellers on price |
| High repeat-rate (~35%) → strong LTV & referral flywheel | Free AI-image apps satisfy some demand digitally |
| Affiliate + eventual branded paint-kit SKU | Likeness/IP/consent legal exposure (minors, celebrities) |
| Corporate/team & seasonal gifting (higher AOV) | Provider/AI pipeline quality or cost volatility |

## 7. Gap Analysis — What's Missing Before You Scale

| Gap | Risk if ignored | Recommended action |
|-----|-----------------|--------------------|
| **CAC vs. margin on the entry tier** | Paid ads to $59 product lose money on first order | Lead ads with the Painted hero; treat Unpainted as organic/repeat/affiliate entry |
| **Painting throughput** | Painted orders bottleneck as volume grows | Lock in Method C (partial-color + detailing) pricing & SLA with PolyX3D; validate on samples |
| **Single fulfillment partner** | One point of failure on capacity, price, quality | Qualify a backup print partner before scaling ad spend |
| **Shipping not in the model** | Silent margin erosion | Decide: baked-in vs. charged separately; recommend baked-in with free-shipping framing above a threshold |
| **No social proof at launch** | Low conversion, high CAC | Seed first orders (friends/family/creators) to generate reviews + UGC before scaling ads |
| **Legal: likeness, minors, IP, refunds** | Takedowns, chargebacks, liability | Finalize terms on consent, minors, celebrity/IP restrictions, and custom-art refund policy before public checkout |
| **LTV/repeat not yet instrumented** | Can't justify CAC without knowing repeat value | Track repeat-rate and referral from order #1; the ~35% category tailwind only helps if measured |
| **Style-drop calendar & retirement rules** | Scarcity promise breaks if inconsistent | Publish a 6-month drop calendar and a firm retirement rule |
| **Capacity planning at scale** | Quality slips under seasonal load (Q4, holidays) | Model peak-week capacity with the partner before running holiday campaigns |

## 8. Recommended Decisions (summary)

1. **Launch two tiers: Unpainted $59, Painted $129.** Test bands $49–69 and $109–149. Present Painted as the default/best value.
2. **Feature the Painted hero in paid ads.** Do not make the $59 product your paid front-end.
3. **Turn on the affiliate thank-you page** for Unpainted buyers (paints, brushes, primer, sealer).
4. **Negotiate Method C** (partial-color print + light artist detailing) with PolyX3D and confirm cost/quality on samples.
5. **Add checkout upsells** (size, rush, gift box, extra figure) to lift AOV toward $110+.
6. **Bake shipping into price** with a free-shipping message; keep the number clean.
7. **Seed reviews/UGC before scaling ads**; instrument the full funnel + repeat-rate from order #1.
8. **Close the legal gaps** (likeness/minors/IP/refunds) before public checkout.
